# Methodology Documentation

## Research Questions
1. What are the primary factors contributing to housing instability among homeless populations?
2. How do demographic variables impact access to housing resources?
3. What role does local policy play in housing availability for at-risk populations?

## Data Sources
- **Census Data:** National and local demographic statistics.
- **Shelter and Housing Services Data:** Information from local shelters and housing assistance programs.
- **Surveys:** Conduct surveys targeting homeless individuals and families to gather firsthand accounts and data.
- **Local Policy Documents:** Review government policies related to housing and homelessness.

## Processing Steps
1. **Data Collection:** Gather data from identified sources, ensuring comprehensive coverage.
2. **Data Cleaning:** Check for missing values, inconsistencies, and outliers. Standardize formats where necessary.
3. **Data Integration:** Merge data from different sources to create a cohesive dataset for analysis.

## Analysis Methods
1. **Descriptive Statistics:** Use to summarize demographic characteristics of the population.
2. **Regression Analysis:** Examine relationships between variables, such as factors affecting housing access.
3. **Qualitative Analysis:** Analyze open-ended survey responses for themes and insights.

## Statistical Approaches
- **Inferential Statistics:** Apply tests to determine the significance of findings (e.g., t-tests, chi-square tests).
- **Predictive Modeling:** Develop models to forecast housing instability based on various indicators.
